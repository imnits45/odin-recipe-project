Egg Dosa

Description

Egg dosa is a popular South Indian dish that combines the goodness of dosa, a fermented rice and lentil crepe, with the richness of eggs.

Ingredients

  • Dosa batter
  • Eggs
  • Salt
  • Oil or ghee (clarified butter)

Steps

  1. Prepare the dosa batter: If you haven't already, soak rice and lentils (usually a mix of urad dal and rice) for a few hours, then grind them into a smooth paste. Ferment this mixture overnight or until it's slightly sour.
  2. Heat a skillet: Place a non-stick skillet or dosa pan on medium heat and let it heat up.
  3. Pour the dosa batter: Once the skillet is hot, pour a ladleful of dosa batter onto the center of the skillet. Using the back of the ladle, spread the batter in a circular motion to form a thin, even layer.
  4. Crack an egg: Crack an egg directly onto the center of the dosa. Be careful not to break the yolk.
  5. Spread the egg: Using a spatula, gently spread the egg over the dosa, being careful not to tear the dosa.
  6. Season with salt: Sprinkle a pinch of salt over the egg to season it.
  7. Drizzle oil or ghee: Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa and on top of the egg.
  8. Cook until done: Let the dosa cook on medium heat until the edges start to brown and crisp up, and the egg is cooked to your desired level of doneness.
  9. Fold and serve: Once the dosa is cooked, fold it in half or roll it up and transfer it to a serving plate.
