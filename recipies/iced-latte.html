Iced Latte

Description

An iced latte is a refreshing coffee beverage that combines espresso with chilled milk and ice. It's a popular choice for those looking for a cool caffeine fix, especially on hot days

Ingredients

  • 1-2 shots of espresso (about 1-2 ounces)
  • 1 cup of cold milk (whole milk, skim milk, almond milk, or any milk of your choice)
  • Ice cubes
  • Optional: sweetener of your choice (sugar, honey, syrup, etc.)

Steps

  1. Brew 1-2 shots of espresso using your preferred method. You can use an espresso machine, a stovetop espresso maker (moka pot), or even instant espresso powder dissolved in hot water.
  2. Once the espresso is ready, let it cool for a few minutes to room temperature. If you prefer a stronger flavor, use 2 shots of espresso; for a milder taste, use 1 shot.
  3. While the espresso is cooling, fill a glass with ice cubes. You can use as much or as little ice as you like, depending on how strong you want your iced latte to be.
  4. Pour the cooled espresso over the ice in the glass.
  5. Next, pour the cold milk into the glass, leaving some space at the top for stirring. You can use any type of milk you prefer, and you can adjust the amount based on your taste preference. For a creamier latte, use more milk; for a stronger coffee flavor, use less milk.
  6. If you like your latte sweetened, you can add a sweetener of your choice at this point. Stir well to dissolve the sweetener into the latte.
  7. Give the iced latte a gentle stir to combine the espresso, milk, and ice.
  8. Taste the latte and adjust the sweetness or milkiness if necessary.
  9. Optional: You can also add flavorings such as vanilla extract, caramel syrup, or chocolate syrup for a flavored iced latte.
  10. Serve immediately and enjoy your homemade iced latte!
